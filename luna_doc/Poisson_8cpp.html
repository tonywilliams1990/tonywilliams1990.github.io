Detailed Description

The equation

\[ \nabla^2 u(x,y) = 2(x^2 + y^2) \]

is solved on the domain \( (x,y) \in [-1,1] \times [-1,1] \) subject to the boundary conditions \( u(-1,y) = e^{-1} \sin(y) + y^2 \), \( u(1,y) = e \sin(y) + y^2 \), \( u(x,-1) = e^x \sin(-1) + x^2 \) and \( u(x,1) = e^x \sin(1) + x^2 \).

The system is solved using a Chebyshev spectral method such that

\[ u(x,y) \approx u_{IJ}(x,y) = \sum_{N=0}^{IJ-1} a_N \Phi_N(x,y) .\]

Here the basis functions \( \Phi_N(x,y) = T_i(x) T_j(y) \) are a product of one-dimensional Chebyshev basis functions with \(i = 0,1,\ldots,I-1 \) and \( j=0,1,\ldots, J-1\) where \( I \) and \( J \) are the number of collocation points in the \( x \) and \( y \) direction respectively. The exact solution is given by

\[ u(x,y) = e^x \sin(y) + x^2 y^2. \]
