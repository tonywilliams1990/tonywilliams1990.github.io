4 /// The equation \f[ u''(x) - (x^6 + 3x^2)u(x) = 0 \f] is solved subject to the
5 /// boundary conditions \f$ u(-1) = u(1) = 1. \f$ The system is solved using a
6 /// Chebyshev spectral method. The solution \f$ u(x) \f$ is symmetric so only
7 /// even Chebyshev polynomials are needed to approximate the solution such that
8 /// \f[ u(x) \approx u_n(x) = \sum_{i=0}^n a_i T_{2i}(x) .\f] Here \f$ n \f$ is
9 /// the number of spectral coefficients \f$ a_i \f$ and \f$ T_{2i}(x) \f$ are
10 /// the corresponding Chebyshev polynomials. The exact solution is given by
11 /// \f[ u(x) = \exp \left[ \frac{x^4 - 1}{4} \right]. \f]
12 /// The spectral solution is found on \f$ x \in [0,1] \f$ and then because the
13 /// solution is symmetric this gives the solution in the entire domain
14 /// \f$ x \in [-1,1]. \f$
