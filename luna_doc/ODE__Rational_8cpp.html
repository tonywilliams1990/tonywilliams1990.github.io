Detailed Description

The equation

\[ u''(y) - u(y) = -2e^{1-y} \]

is solved on \( y \in [0,\infty)\) subject to the boundary conditions \( u(0) = e\) and \( u(y) \to 0 \) as \( y \to \infty\).

The system is solved using rational Chebyshev functions on a semi-infinite interval \( TL_i(y; L) \). The solution \( u(y) \) is approximated by

\[ u(y) \approx u_n(y) = \sum_{i=0}^n a_i TL_{i}(y; L) .\]

Here \( n \) is the number of spectral coefficients \( a_i \) and \( L \) is the map parameter associated with the rational Chebyshev functions \( TL_i(y; L) \). The exact solution is given by

\[ u(y) = (y+1) e^{1-y}. \]

The spectral solution is found on \( y \in [0,y_{\infty}] \) where \( y_{\infty} \) is determined by the number of spectral coefficients. For more information on rational Chebyshev functions on a semi-infinite interval see Boyd, J.P., 1987. Orthogonal rational functions on a semi-infinite interval. Journal of Computational Physics, 70(1), pp.63-88 .
