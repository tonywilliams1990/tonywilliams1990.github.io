4 /// The equation \f[ u''(y) - u(y) = -2e^{1-y} \f] is solved on
5 /// \f$ y \in [0,\infty)\f$ subject to the boundary conditions \f$ u(0) = e\f$
6 /// and \f$ u(y) \to 0 \f$ as \f$ y \to \infty\f$. The system is solved using
7 /// rational Chebyshev functions on a semi-infinite interval \f$ TL_i(y; L) \f$.
8 /// The solution \f$ u(y) \f$ is approximated by
9 /// \f[ u(y) \approx u_n(y) = \sum_{i=0}^n a_i TL_{i}(y; L) .\f]
10 /// Here \f$ n \f$ is the number of spectral coefficients \f$ a_i \f$ and
11 /// \f$ L \f$ is the map parameter associated with the rational Chebyshev
12 /// functions \f$ TL_i(y; L) \f$. The exact solution is given by
13 /// \f[ u(y) = (y+1) e^{1-y}. \f]
14 /// The spectral solution is found on \f$ y \in [0,y_{\infty}] \f$ where
15 /// \f$ y_{\infty} \f$ is determined by the number of spectral coefficients. For
16 /// more information on rational Chebyshev functions on a semi-infinite interval
17 /// see <b> Boyd, J.P., 1987. Orthogonal rational functions on a semi-infinite
18 /// interval. Journal of Computational Physics, 70(1), pp.63-88 </b>.
