3 /// Solve Laplace's equation \f[ \nabla^2 u = 0, \f] on the unit square
4 /// \f$ (x,y) \in [0,1] \times [0,1] \f$. The equation is subject to the
5 /// boundary conditions \f[ u(x,0) = 0, \hspace{0.5cm}
6 /// u(x,1) = \frac{1}{(1+x)^2 + 1}, \hspace{0.5cm} 0 \leq x \leq 1, \f]
7 /// \f[ u(0,y) = \frac{y}{1+y^2}, \hspace{0.5cm} u(1,y) = \frac{y}{4+y^2},
8 /// \hspace{0.5cm} 0 \leq y \leq 1. \f] The exact solution is given by \f[
9 /// u(x,y) = \frac{y}{(1+x)^2 + y^2}, \hspace{0.5cm} 0 \leq x,y \leq 1. \f]
10 /// The equation is discretised using a second order finite-difference scheme
11 /// with step size \f$ \Delta x = \Delta y = 1 / N \f$ where \f$ N \f$ is the
12 /// number of intervals. The internal nodes in the discretisation are governed
13 /// by the finite-difference equation \f[ u_{i-1,j} + u_{i,j-1} - 4u_{i,j} +
14 /// u_{i,j+1} + u_{i+1,j} = 0, \f] where \f$ u(x,y) \approx u(x_i,y_j) =
15 /// u_{i,j} \f$ and \f$ x_i = i \Delta x, \hspace{0.2cm} y_j = j \Delta y \f$
16 /// with \f$ i,j = 0,1,\ldots,N. \f$
