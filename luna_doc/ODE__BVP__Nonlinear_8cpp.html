Detailed Description

The equation

\[ u(x) u''(x) - \left( u'(x) \right)^2 = -1 \]

is solved subject to the boundary conditions \( u(1) = \frac{1}{2} \left(e - e^{-1} \right) = - u(-1). \) The system is solved using a Chebyshev spectral method.

The solution \( u(x) \) is anti-symmetric so only odd Chebyshev polynomials are needed to approximate the solution such that

\[ u(x) \approx u_n(x) = \sum_{i=0}^n a_i T_{2i+1}(x) .\]

Here \( n \) is the number of spectral coefficients \( a_i \) and \( T_{2i+1}(x) \) are the corresponding Chebyshev polynomials. The solution to this nonlinear equation is found by splitting it into a known part \( u^g(x) \) and a correction \( u^c(x) \), such that \( u(x) = u^g(x) + u^c(x)\) , and using Newton iteration to update the known part until convergence. The correction is found from the resulting linearised equations. The exact solution is given by \( u(x) = \sinh(x). \) The spectral solution is found on \( x \in [0,1] \) and then because the solution is anti-symmetric this gives the solution in the entire domain \( x \in [-1,1]. \)
