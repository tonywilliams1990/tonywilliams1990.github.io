Detailed Description

The equation

\[ u''(x) - (x^6 + 3x^2)u(x) = 0 \]

is solved subject to the boundary conditions \( u(-1) = u(1) = 1. \) The system is solved using a Chebyshev spectral method.

The solution \( u(x) \) is symmetric so only even Chebyshev polynomials are needed to approximate the solution such that

\[ u(x) \approx u_n(x) = \sum_{i=0}^n a_i T_{2i}(x) .\]

Here \( n \) is the number of spectral coefficients \( a_i \) and \( T_{2i}(x) \) are the corresponding Chebyshev polynomials. The exact solution is given by

\[ u(x) = \exp \left[ \frac{x^4 - 1}{4} \right]. \]

The spectral solution is found on \( x \in [0,1] \) and then because the solution is symmetric this gives the solution in the entire domain \( x \in [-1,1]. \)
