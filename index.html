Current research

Injection into boundary layers: solutions beyond the classical form

This study examines three-dimensional boundary-layer solutions for laminar incompressible uniform flow along a flat plate with an injection, of limited spanwise extent, through the plate surface. Retaining viscous diffusion in both the spanwise and transverse directions allows for a fully three-dimensional boundary layer formulation. Using a finite-width slot injection over a spanwise lengthscale which is comparable to the boundary layer thickness we see the formation of low-speed streaks. Unlike the extensively studied two-dimensional case three-dimensional solutions appear to be possible for any injection rate with the geometry of the streaks being dependent upon the injection amplitude and the width of the injection slot. By taking the limit of large injection slot width an asymptotic description provides a means of classifying the solutions depending upon the fixed injection rate.
